An Explanatory Note

My goals in creating this document are three-fold. The first is to provide what Kinbote was unable to: a legitimate commentary to John Shade's posthumous masterpiece. To that end, I will be glossing some of Shade's more obscure references and allusions (at least insofar as I am able to identify them), highlighting the particularly clever poetic devices that are easily overshadowed (overshaded?) by Kinbote's narrative, and chronicling an array of scholarly opinions with regard to the literary merit of the poetic text.

My second goal is to provide a sort of meta-commentary on Kinbote's own annotations. This will focus both on Kinbote's work as it stands on its own as well as its relation to Shade's poem. As with my notes to the poem, this commentary will contain both my own observations and those of scholarly critics far more knowledgeable than myself.

My final goal is to embrace the absurdity of attempting to interpret a novel that seems designed to confound interpretation. This is by no means a comprehensive analysis of Nabokov's work, nor can I guarantee that my analyses align with Nabokov's intent. This work is meant chiefly to engage with the playfulness of the novel and to provide what I feel are interesting insights, although anything I have to say will pale in comparison to Nabokov's fire.

These pages contain my original comments, as well as the complete text of John Shade's poem and relevant excerpts from Kinbote's commentary. Cross-references have been hyper-linked to facilitate following. Additionally, all links have been color-coded. Azure is used for links to the poem, royal purple is reserved for Kinbote's text, and green links redirect to my own contributions. References to Shade and Kinbote are consistent with the strictly literal interpretation in which John Shade is a reclusive New England poet and Charles Kinbote is his eccentric neighbor and commentator. As will be discussed, however, this is far from the only (or even the most interesting) possible reading of the novel (a brief run-down of the various theories of authorship discussed in this analysis can be found here).

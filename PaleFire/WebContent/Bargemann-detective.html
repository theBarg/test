A rare moment of correctness for Kinbote, as there is indeed no Sherlock Holmes story concerning reversed footprints. That being said, there is still a characteristic ring of strangeness about this note. For one thing, a gloss for such a famous character is unnecessary for the presumably American literary audience Kinbote is ostensibly writing for, and its inclusion speaks more to Kinbote's unfamiliarity with English-speaking culture than anything else. Additionally, it feels somewhat out of place as one of the only strictly informative notes in the entire novel.

This note can be seen as an early hint from Nabokov to the reader concerning Pale Fire's identity as a sort of deconstructed mystery novel, in which the reader themself serves as detective (see also my note to Canto Two, line 368).

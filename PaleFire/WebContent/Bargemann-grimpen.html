Hazel appears to be reading The Hound of the Baskervilles, in which Grimpen is a treacherous bog not unlike the one Hazel herself will end her life in. Shade must have chosen this reference intentionally as foreshadowing for Canto Two's closing lines, but in a metatextual sense this is another hint that Pale Fire is, in fact, a detective story (see my note to Kinbote's note to Canto One, line 27).

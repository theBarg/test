Nabokov scholar Maurice Couturier provides, in a rather ironic instance of life imitating art, the following variant to this line gleaned from Nabokov's manuscript:

(Indeed it sold exceptionally well-
Mainly because I had seen fit to dwell
In scolarly (sic) detail on the delights
Byron devised from little catamites)

These lines certainly seem out of character for Shade, whose poem is otherwise entirely chaste. Additionally, delights devised from catamites seem much more up Kinbote's alley than Shade's. Nabokov may have originally intended for these lines to appear as though they were inserted by Kinbote, or they could simply represent an earlier direction for the text that he ended up veering away from.

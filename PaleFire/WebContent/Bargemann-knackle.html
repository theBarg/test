After an extensive search, the only definition of "knackle" I have come across (aside from a smattering of hits on Urban Dictionary, most of which appear to be a typo for "kankle") is this one, from the 1883 A Glossary of the Dialect of Almondbury and Huddersfield edited by the Reverend Thomas Lees. In that text, knackle is defined as "to mend in a small way ; to trifle, &c." So what exactly does Kinbote mean here? Does he mean that they will only eat a triflingly small amount of walnuts? Is he simply a confused foreigner misusing a rather obscure word? For that matter, though, one must wonder how he would even have come across a word that appears to be defined only in an obscure 19th century British slang dictionary.

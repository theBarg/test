Surprisingly, Kinbote's attribution is in fact correct. Just how sexual the Erlking's fascination with the boy is varies with different translations, and I am not well-versed enough in German to make any claim of my own. However, it does seem rather likely that in his description of the Erlking's motives, Kinbote is reading in some of his own predatory nature.

It is probably worth noting that in Franz Schubert's hypnotic musical setting of the poem (a performance of which can be found here), a single soloist sings all four characters, using different vocal ranges to differentiate between them. This idea of one creator lending voice to several unique characters is similar to the reading of the novel in which Kinbote is simply Shade's creation.
